Development of Innovative Simulation Methods

We create next-generation molecular- and quantum-level simulation algorithms that bridge time and length scales impossible with conventional approaches.

Highlighted sub-projects

  • Machine-Learning Molecular Dynamics (ML-MD): Coupling graph-neural-network force fields with active-learning pipelines.
  • Multiscale Ion Transport: Hybrid continuum–atomistic solvers for Å-scale nanopores.
  • Ultra-Fast Free-Energy Sampling: GPU-native adaptive-bias schemes that converge 10× faster.
Ionic Neuromorphic Computing

Inspired by biology, we design electrochemical circuit elements—ionic memristors, memcapacitors, and synaptic transistors—that operate with sub-picojoule switching energies.

Highlighted sub-projects

  • Å-Scale Memristors: Voltage-gated nanopores exhibiting long-term plasticity.
  • Ion-Electrode Intercalation: Ab initio simulations of redox-mediated learning rules.
  • Circuit-to-Device Co-Design: Mapping spiking neural networks onto electrochemical hardware.
Nanopore-Based Molecular Separation

We leverage Å-precision pores and bio-inspired surface chemistry to selectively extract hydrogen and critical rare-earth elements at dramatically lower energy cost.

Highlighted sub-projects

  • Selective Hydrogen Sieving: Free-energy-landscape engineering for H2/CO2 separations.
  • Rare-Earth Ion Capture: Tailored pore functionalization guided by DFT-derived descriptors.
  • Operando Spectroscopy: Combining in-situ Raman with MD to resolve transport pathways.
